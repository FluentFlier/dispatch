# Design: Objective-Driven Leads Without Setup Friction

## Problem Statement

Content OS currently treats a lead as a company discovered in a startup directory, followed by a founder-resolution step. That works for one narrow sales motion. It fails when the user wants to raise money, recruit, find partners, build a network, identify advisors, or reach a specific kind of person.

The revamp must let a user describe any outcome, use their website and other evidence to understand their situation, discover people and/or organizations, explain why each target fits, and pass every qualified target through the existing feed, drafting, approval, outreach, and nurture workflow.

The product must become more capable without becoming a larger form. Users should supply one outcome and one strong source of truth. Content OS should infer the rest, show its work, and ask only when ambiguity materially changes the search.

## Demand Evidence

No customer interviews, usage recordings, conversion metrics, or payment evidence were provided in this session. The repository does contain a large working lead surface, outreach lifecycle, lead imports, enrichment, ICP profiles, engagement signals, and nurture automation. That proves product investment and an existing workflow, but it does not prove which new objective users need most.

The implementation should instrument Mission creation, time-to-first-workable-lead, corrections, dismiss reasons, saves, and outreach. The first release supports the two objectives explicitly required here, customer acquisition and fundraising. The strategy interface must allow later hiring, partnership, networking, and custom strategies, but those strategies do not ship until observed demand and provider quality justify them.

## Status Quo

The current user experience is:

1. Global onboarding automatically pushes the user into LinkedIn/X connection.
2. Creator data is ingested to build a voice baseline.
3. Leads Setup asks, “Who do you sell to?” in an ICP chat.
4. Discovery extracts companies, primarily from startup directories and company searches.
5. The system tries to resolve a founder or executive contact.
6. The user reviews a company card, drafts outreach, approves or sends it, and moves the lead through nurture.

The highest-friction parts are not the number of clicks alone. They are conceptual:

- Users must translate their goal into an ICP even when they are not selling.
- The system asks users for facts it could infer from their website or profile.
- “Setup,” “scrape,” “ICP,” and “directory” expose implementation concepts instead of the user’s job.
- A result can look like a lead without a grounded reason, current trigger, or reachable action.
- A single active ICP cannot cleanly represent simultaneous fundraising and sales work.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

The target user is a founder, creator, operator, or independent professional who knows the outcome they want but does not know exactly whom to approach.

Examples:

- “Raise a $2M seed round from fintech and creator-economy investors.”
- “Find heads of growth at 20–100 person B2B SaaS companies.”
- “Recruit senior founding engineers with agent infrastructure experience.”
- “Find podcast hosts and newsletter writers who cover creator tools.”
- “Build relationships with other founders selling to the same audience.”

The narrowest useful wedge is:

> One sentence describing the outcome, one source of truth about the user, and five grounded targets with an evidence-backed next action.

No integrations, saved filters, delivery schedules, or automation are required before the first five targets appear.

## Product Thesis

### The unit of work is a Mission, not an ICP

An ICP describes a market segment. A Mission describes what the user is trying to make happen now. It contains an outcome, target hypothesis, constraints, and success event.

“Seed-stage fintech companies” is an ICP.

“Book five demos with compliance leaders at seed-stage fintech companies this month” is a Mission.

The Mission gives discovery, scoring, copy, and next-action selection enough context to behave differently without requiring different lead products.

### Durable context and temporary intent must be separated

Global onboarding should collect durable context:

- What the user/company builds
- Website, LinkedIn profile, deck, document, or pasted description
- Product, customer, stage, geography, traction, and proof inferred from those sources
- User-confirmed corrections only where confidence is low

Leads should collect temporary intent:

- Desired outcome
- People, organizations, or both
- Time horizon and success event
- Must-have constraints and exclusions

The website belongs in onboarding because it helps content, voice, and leads. A fundraising target belongs in Leads because it may be replaced by a sales target next week.

### A lead is a workable opportunity, not a database row

A target is not shown as “workable” unless the system has:

1. A grounded identity: a real person or organization with a canonical source.
2. Mission fit: a specific explanation tied to the user’s current Mission.
3. Evidence: citations for the key fit claims.
4. Timing: a current signal when one exists, or an honest “no timing signal found.”
5. Actionability: a reachable route or a concrete research/nurture action.

Missing data is visible. Confidence is not converted into fake certainty. A result that does not meet this contract is a **candidate**, not a workable lead. Candidates may appear in a separate research state, but they cannot be drafted, approved, or sent until the missing contract is satisfied.

### Normative workable-lead contract

A result may enter the actionable feed only when all of the following are true:

- Identity: one verified source identity, or two independent public sources that agree on the entity.
- Fit: at least two distinct Mission-specific fit claims have claim-level citations. The claims may come from one authoritative source when separate captured excerpts explicitly support each claim; two independent sources are not mandatory. Provider benchmarking must measure how this rule affects workable yield.
- Timing: a cited signal observed within the objective strategy's freshness window, or an explicit `no_current_signal` value. Timing is never silently inferred.
- Action: one channel-eligible contact route or one non-send next action with a cited public surface, such as a recent post to engage with.
- Evidence: every fact used by scoring or drafting has source URL, captured excerpt/hash, observation time, and confidence.
- Safety: the target is not suppressed and the proposed action passes channel and workspace policy.

Anything below this bar remains a candidate. The product may say “3 workable leads and 7 candidates,” but it may not present ten equally actionable cards.

## Friction Model

Every requested field must pass one test: would a wrong inference change who we search for or what action we recommend?

If no, infer it and let the user edit later.

### Global onboarding

The first release puts this prompt on the first Leads visit, before the Mission composer, so the value and abandonment impact can be measured without changing global onboarding. After the prompt shows neutral or positive activation impact, promote the same component into global onboarding behind a separate experiment flag.

Replace the automatic social-connect redirect only in that later experiment with one optional prompt:

> What are you building or growing?

Accept any one of:

- Website URL
- LinkedIn profile or company URL
- Deck/document URL
- File upload
- Pasted text
- One-sentence description

Primary action: **Analyze and continue**

Secondary action: **Skip for now**

Do not show separate fields for company name, industry, audience, product, stage, or geography before analysis. Extract those into a context preview. Ask at most one follow-up when two interpretations would produce materially different lead searches.

Social connection remains an optional enrichment step. It improves voice, warm-path detection, verification, and outreach, but it must not block entry into the app or the first lead search.

### First visit to Leads

Show a single composer:

> What are you trying to make happen?

The input accepts plain language. Under it, show context-aware suggestions such as:

- Find customers
- Raise a round

Release one shows only **Find customers** and **Raise a round**. The user may still type anything, but unsupported objectives are identified before confirmation and saved as draft requests rather than pretending discovery can run them.

Content OS produces a one-card Mission preview:

- Outcome
- Who/what to find
- Constraints inferred from context
- What counts as success
- Sources it will search

The default action is **Find leads**. Editing is optional. Discovery begins immediately after confirmation and streams partial results into the familiar feed.

### Returning visits

The selected Mission appears as a compact bar above the feed. The default feed contains actionable matches for that Mission only. “All Missions” is an explicit filter that aggregates running Missions, groups duplicate entities, shows a badge and unread count per Mission, and opens the match belonging to the selected card. Switching Missions clears the selected detail item unless the same match exists in the destination Mission.

Users can switch, pause, duplicate, or create a Mission without visiting a Setup page.

Store selected Mission per user and workspace in `user_settings` as a Mission ID plus selection time. If it is paused, keep it selected but show the paused state. If archived or deleted, fall back to the newest running Mission, then newest draft, then the empty composer. A user can always select All Missions explicitly.

Advanced controls remain available behind **Tune search**, including sources, geography, exclusions, schedules, daily caps, and delivery channels.

“Setup” becomes “Missions” or “Tune search.” “Scrape now” becomes “Find leads.” “ICP” is retained only in internal code and migration notes.

## Core Interaction

The feed keeps its existing mental model:

1. Select a result.
2. Understand who/what it is.
3. See why it fits and why now.
4. Review the recommended next action and draft.
5. Approve, rewrite, save, dismiss, or nurture.

The visible hierarchy changes:

### Feed card

- Target display name
- Person or Organization badge
- Associated organization/person as secondary text
- Mission-fit score
- One-line “why now” or “why fit”
- Reachability state
- Warm-path indicator when present

### Detail panel

- Target identity and verified links
- Mission name
- “Why this is workable” evidence list with citations
- Confidence and freshness per claim
- Recommended next action
- Editable draft
- Existing outreach and nurture controls

Company facts, photos, directories, and source-specific metadata move below the mission evidence. The user is deciding whether to act, not browsing a company encyclopedia.

### Feedback

Dismiss asks for an optional one-tap reason:

- Wrong person
- Wrong organization
- Wrong stage/size
- Not relevant to this Mission
- Weak evidence
- Already know them
- No contact path

These signals create a proposed Mission adjustment. One dismissal never changes search behavior. After three consistent reasons, or two reasons affecting more than 40% of reviewed results, the UI proposes the exact constraint change with Accept and Reject actions. Accepted changes create a new immutable MissionSpec version, retain an audit record, and can be reverted. They do not silently rewrite durable company context. A dismissal without a reason changes only that match's status.

## Entity Model

### User-facing concepts

- **Context:** Stable facts about the user, company, product, and proof.
- **Mission:** A current outcome and target hypothesis.
- **Target:** A real person or organization.
- **Lead:** A target matched to a Mission, with evidence and workflow state.

The same target may be a lead in several Missions with different reasons and next actions. Maya Chen can be a fundraising lead today and a partnership lead later. Her identity should be canonical; her Mission match should not be.

### Storage approach

Use an additive compatibility migration.

#### 1. Context sources

Create `lead_context_sources`:

- `id`, `workspace_id`
- `kind`: website, linkedin, deck, document, text, social_profile
- `url`, `raw_text_hash`, `title`
- `extracted_context jsonb`
- `confidence jsonb`
- `status`, `error`
- `fetched_at`, `created_at`, `updated_at`

Create `lead_context_claims` as the authoritative synthesized context store:

- `id`, `workspace_id`, `claim_key`, `value jsonb`, `schema_version`
- `source_id`, `source_excerpt_hash`, `confidence`
- `user_override boolean`, `invalidated_at`
- `created_at`, `updated_at`

User overrides win over extracted claims. Re-fetching a source creates or invalidates source-derived claims but never overwrites an explicit user correction. Claim changes enqueue Mission recompilation and asynchronous re-scoring only when the changed claim is referenced by that Mission. The existing Brain/GTM page is a read projection of these claims, not a second authority. Projection writes are prohibited; failed projection syncs retry from the claim log.

Use `lead_mission_context_sources(mission_id, context_source_id)` rather than JSON source IDs so RLS, deletion, and referential integrity remain enforceable.

#### 2. Missions

Keep `signal_icp_profiles` during migration, but make **Mission** the only user-facing name. Add only stable, queryable columns:

- `goal_statement text`
- `strategy_type`: customer_acquisition or fundraising in release one
- `success_event text`
- `status`: draft, running, paused, archived
- `last_run_at`

Create immutable `lead_mission_versions`:

- `id`, `mission_id`, `version_number`, `schema_version`
- `compiled_spec jsonb`, validated by the versioned MissionSpec schema
- `compiler_version`, `created_by_user_id`, `created_at`

Create normalized `lead_mission_criteria` rows for frequently queried constraints and exclusions:

- `mission_version_id`, `field`, `operator`, `value_text`, `value_number`, `value_jsonb`
- `is_exclusion`, `source`: user, inferred, or feedback_proposal

JSONB remains only for sparse strategy-specific extensions and always includes `schema_version`.

Existing fields remain useful:

- `goal_type` provides a coarse default strategy.
- `target_personas`, `verticals`, and `keywords` seed the structured target model.
- `pitch_angle`, `tone_rules`, and daily caps continue to drive outreach.

Replace the single-active-Mission constraint with a selected/default Mission preference. Several Missions may run; one may be selected in the UI.

#### 3. Mission runs

Create `lead_mission_runs` so execution is resumable and attributable:

- `id`, `mission_id`, `mission_version_id`, `workspace_id`
- `status`: queued, running, cancelling, cancelled, completed, partial, failed
- `idempotency_key`, unique per Mission version and explicit trigger
- `search_budget`, `spent_budget`, `result_limit`
- `cursor jsonb`, `checkpoint_at`, `attempt`, `max_attempts`
- `started_at`, `completed_at`, `cancelled_at`, `error_summary`

A run always references an immutable Mission version. Editing a Mission creates a new version and optionally starts a new run; it never mutates an active run. Cancellation stops new provider work, preserves already-grounded results, and marks unfinished candidates cancelled. Retries resume from adapter checkpoints and do not duplicate matches. Recurring schedules create new runs only when the prior run is terminal. Release one permits one running run per Mission and three per workspace.

#### 4. Source identities and linked entities

Create `signal_lead_entities`:

- `id`, `workspace_id`
- `entity_type`: person or organization
- `resolution_state`: source_only, suggested_link, linked, disputed
- `display_name`
- `canonical_url`
- `domain`
- `headline`, `location`
- `attributes jsonb`
- `source_links jsonb`
- `first_seen_at`, `last_seen_at`

Create `signal_lead_source_identities` with a unique `(workspace_id, source, external_id, entity_type)` key. Entity linking is confidence-based and reversible. Release one auto-links only exact verified provider IDs or exact normalized domains for organizations. All name-based and cross-source person links remain `suggested_link` until a user or deterministic provider identifier confirms them.

Store aliases and every merge decision with provenance. A merge never destroys source identities. Manual split restores prior entity IDs and reattaches Mission matches. Common-name collisions remain separate by default. Release one models only person-to-organization affiliation relationships needed by sales and fundraising; generalized organization graphs are deferred.

Entity merges lock both entities and their matches in one transaction. The higher-trust entity survives; ties use the older entity ID. The removed ID becomes an immutable redirect. When both entities have a match in the same Mission version, the higher workflow state survives as the active match, while notes, messages, state events, citations, and send-ledger entries from both remain attached through a merge-history link. Drafts never merge automatically; the newer unsent draft is retained as an alternate. Identity-global saves and suppressions are unioned, Mission-fit dismissals remain in history, and conversion/open-conversation state wins over uncontacted state. Reversible split uses the merge manifest to restore entity and match ownership without deleting audit history.

Create `signal_lead_affiliations`:

- `id`, `workspace_id`, `person_entity_id`, `organization_entity_id`
- `role`, `started_at`, `ended_at`, `is_current`
- `confidence`, `source_identity_id`
- `created_at`, `updated_at`

Evidence attaches through `signal_lead_affiliation_evidence(affiliation_id, entity_evidence_id)`. Create `signal_lead_entity_evidence` as the Mission-independent evidence authority for identity, aliases, domains, and affiliations. It stores workspace, entity/source identity, source URL/provider record, sanitized excerpt or permitted summary, excerpt hash, captured time, retention class, and invalidation time. Match-level evidence references entity evidence when reused and stores only Mission-specific claim linkage. Entity evidence survives match deletion but follows source deletion, workspace deletion, and retention rules. Unique current affiliation is not assumed; investors and advisors may have several. Duplicate rows are prevented by `(workspace_id, person_entity_id, organization_entity_id, normalized_role, coalesce(started_at, sentinel))`. Conflicting current affiliations remain separate with confidence and evidence rather than overwriting history.

#### 5. Mission matches

Retain `signal_leads` as the workflow-compatible Mission match record and add:

- `mission_id`, `mission_version_id`, `mission_run_id`
- `entity_id`
- `target_type`
- `target_name`
- `associated_organization_name`
- `canonical_url`
- `why_fit text`
- `why_now text`
- `confidence numeric`
- `freshness_at`
- `result_state`: discovered, grounded, qualifying, candidate, workable_research, workable_outreach, stale, suppressed, cancelled, failed
- `fit_score`, `timing_score`, `reachability_score`, `evidence_score`, `rank_score`
- `score_version`, `action_type`, `action_eligible`, `stale_reason`, `suppression_id`
- `state_changed_at`, `state_changed_by`, `state_change_reason`

Create relational `signal_lead_claims` and `signal_lead_evidence` tables. Each claim records its type, normalized value, observation time, confidence, score eligibility, and one or more evidence rows with URL/provider record, excerpt hash, and captured time. Drafting and scoring may read only score-eligible claims.

Create `signal_lead_state_events` as the append-only transition audit. State updates and audit insertion occur in one transaction. `signal_leads` stores the current projection used by the feed.

Match uniqueness is `(workspace_id, mission_version_id, entity_id)`. Rediscovery in the same version updates the existing match idempotently. A new Mission version creates a new match while linking `supersedes_lead_id` to the prior match. Identity-level saves carry forward. Dismissal carries forward only when its reason is identity-global, such as already known or do-not-contact. Drafts, Mission-fit dismissals, scores, why-fit/why-now, next action, and nurture plan do not carry forward. Sent/replied/conversion history remains on the old match and is visible from the entity timeline. An open conversation blocks a new outreach match through the recipient ledger.

Existing outreach, notes, events, messages, nurture, and conversion state remain attached to `signal_leads.id`. This preserves the full working loop.

For compatibility during rollout:

- Existing rows backfill as `target_type = organization`, `target_name = company_name`.
- New organization targets continue populating `company_name`.
- A person name is never written into `company_name`. Person writes stay disabled until every legacy consumer is entity-aware and the column is nullable. Associated organization, when known, may populate `company_name` only as organization context.
- Person targets create a primary `signal_lead_contacts` row so current verification and send paths continue to work after the consumer gate passes.
- Central helpers replace direct display assumptions: `leadDisplayName`, `leadOrganizationName`, `leadPrimaryPerson`, and `leadCanonicalUrl`.

This transitional duplication is intentional. It keeps current routes working while the UI and pipeline move to typed targets. Direct `company_name` reads must be removed from all consumer paths before person discovery can write production rows.

All cross-table tenant references use composite foreign keys that include `workspace_id`, backed by unique `(workspace_id, id)` constraints. RLS is defense in depth, not the only protection against cross-workspace Mission, entity, source, evidence, affiliation, or match links.

## Discovery Architecture

### Mission compiler

Compile plain language plus durable context into a validated `MissionSpec`:

- Objective
- Target entity types
- Target roles/personas
- Organization properties
- Geography
- Stage, size, check size, seniority, or other objective-specific criteria
- Positive signals
- Exclusions
- Success event
- Search budget and source plan

Show the compiled summary to the user, not the internal JSON.

Release-one `MissionSpecV1` is a versioned, Zod-validated contract:

```ts
type MissionSpecV1 = {
  schemaVersion: 1;
  strategy: 'customer_acquisition' | 'fundraising';
  goalStatement: string;
  targetEntityTypes: Array<'person' | 'organization'>;
  personas: Array<{ role: string; seniority?: string }>;
  organization: {
    industries?: string[];
    stages?: string[];
    employeeMin?: number;
    employeeMax?: number;
    checkSizeMin?: number;
    checkSizeMax?: number;
  };
  geographies: string[];
  successEvent: 'qualified_reply' | 'meeting_booked' | 'intro_received';
  positiveSignals: string[];
  exclusions: Array<{ field: string; operator: string; value: unknown }>;
  assumptions: Array<{ field: string; value: unknown; confidence: number; sourceClaimId?: string }>;
};
```

Required fields are strategy, goal statement, at least one entity type, and success event. Ask one follow-up only when a missing field changes the adapter family, recipient type, or eligibility threshold. Examples: fund versus angel; company versus independent buyer; investor check size that excludes most candidates. Geography, exact industry, and timing default to broad values and appear as editable assumptions. Unsupported objectives are saved as draft Missions with an honest “strategy not available yet” state; they do not silently reuse the wrong strategy.

### Objective-aware query planner

The current web discovery appends “companies list,” which guarantees company-shaped results. Replace it with a query planner:

- Fundraising searches both firms and individual partners/angels, then connects people to thesis, check size, stage, and portfolio evidence.
- Customer acquisition may search organizations first and resolve decision makers, or search people first when the buyer is an independent professional.
- Hiring searches people, work history, authored work, communities, and warm paths.
- Partnerships searches both organizations and the people who own the relevant function.
- Networking prioritizes people, shared context, mutual engagement, and credible reasons to connect.

Adapters return a generalized `DiscoveredTarget`, not `IngestedLead`:

- Identity fields
- Entity type
- Associated entities
- Source URL
- Evidence snippets
- Observed timestamp
- Candidate contact routes

Each strategy implements the same interface: `compile`, `planQueries`, `qualify`, `rank`, `recommendAction`, and `freshnessPolicy`. Release one ships customer acquisition and fundraising. Hiring, partnerships, and networking are registered only after provider and demand gates pass.

### Result state machine

- `discovered`: source identity exists, not yet grounded
- `grounded`: identity contract passed
- `qualifying`: fit claims are being fetched and evaluated
- `candidate`: identity is grounded but the workable contract is incomplete
- `workable_research`: identity, fit, evidence, and safety passed; only a cited non-send action is eligible
- `workable_outreach`: identity, fit, evidence, safety, and an eligible recipient channel passed
- `stale`: a required claim exceeded its strategy freshness window
- `suppressed`: do-not-contact, deletion, policy, or workspace rule blocks action
- `cancelled`: the parent run stopped before the match became workable; partial evidence is retained for a future run
- `failed`: terminal provider or parsing failure with retry metadata

Both workable states appear in the default actionable feed. Only `workable_outreach` permits message drafting and approval. `workable_research` permits approving a research, follow, save, or public-engagement task. Candidates appear behind a separate “Needs research” filter. Stale matches remain visible to users who already acted on them but cannot start new outreach until refreshed. Suppressed matches never expose send controls. Cancelling a run moves only its non-workable, unfinished matches to `cancelled`; already workable matches remain workable and keep run attribution.

### Grounding

Preserve and extend the existing hallucination guard:

- Every target name must appear in a fetched source or verified platform result.
- Every qualification claim must cite a source block.
- Search snippets may identify candidates but high-impact claims require a fetched page or platform record.
- Unsupported claims are removed, not softened into plausible language.
- Conflicting claims are retained with source dates and shown as uncertain.
- Stale claims are labeled.

The ranking model may infer fit from grounded facts. It may not invent the facts it ranks.

Release-one claim/evidence payloads are also versioned:

```ts
type LeadClaimV1 = {
  schemaVersion: 1;
  claimType: 'role' | 'affiliation' | 'industry' | 'stage' | 'check_size' | 'thesis' | 'portfolio' | 'intent_signal' | 'contact_route';
  value: string | number | { min?: number; max?: number; currency?: string };
  observedAt: string;
  confidence: number; // 0..1
  scoreEligible: boolean;
  evidenceIds: string[];
};

type LeadEvidenceV1 = {
  schemaVersion: 1;
  sourceType: 'provider_record' | 'web_page' | 'social_post' | 'user_source';
  sourceUrl?: string;
  providerRecordId?: string;
  excerpt: string; // capped, sanitized, and policy-permitted
  excerptHash: string;
  capturedAt: string;
};
```

Unknown schema versions are retained but excluded from scoring and drafting until a registered reader migrates or understands them.

### Ranking

Separate four scores:

- `fit_score`: match to Mission criteria
- `timing_score`: strength and freshness of a current trigger
- `reachability_score`: verified contact route or warm path
- `evidence_score`: source coverage and confidence

All component scores are 0–100. Missing components score zero, not a neutral midpoint. Release-one weights are versioned:

- Customer acquisition: fit 40%, timing 25%, reachability 20%, evidence 15%.
- Fundraising: fit 50%, timing 15%, reachability 15%, evidence 20%.

Identity and safety are gates, not score components. A match becomes workable only with `evidence_score >= 70`, `fit_score >= 65`, and a valid action. `rank_score` breaks ties by evidence score, then freshness, then stable entity ID. Every persisted score stores `score_version`, component values, missing-value flags, and computed time. Initial thresholds are configuration, covered by offline fixtures, and recalibrated only through a versioned change.

Initial deterministic component calculations:

- Evidence score: weighted coverage of required claim groups. Identity 25, role/affiliation 20, objective-specific fit 35, action/timing 20. A group earns `weight × minimum(claim confidence in group)`. Conflicting evidence caps that group at 50% until resolved.
- Reachability score: verified direct channel 100; verified warm path 90; verified public engagement surface 70; unverified direct route 40; no route 0.
- Timing score: qualifying signal within half its freshness window 100; within the full window 70; explicit no-current-signal 30; stale or unsupported 0.
- Fit score, organization-first customer acquisition: organization criteria 40%, persona authority 35%, positive signal match 25%. Missing persona authority caps fit at 60.
- Fit score, independent-person customer acquisition: persona/problem fit 50%, authority or purchasing autonomy 25%, positive signal match 25%. Organization criteria are not required.
- Fit score, VC fund/partner fundraising: stage/check-size 35%, thesis/portfolio overlap 40%, investor role 25%. Missing check-size evidence caps fit at 60.
- Fit score, angel fundraising: relevant operator/investment history 35%, thesis/domain overlap 40%, active-investor evidence 25%. Public check size is optional and affects ranking, not workability.

Each scoring profile is independently versioned and has at least ten positive, ten borderline, ten conflicting, and ten negative fixtures with expected component-score ranges and result states. The provider benchmark reports workable yield separately for organization buyers, independent buyers, VC funds/partners, and angels.

Release-one freshness defaults, owned by `score_version`:

- Role and current affiliation: 90 days
- Fund thesis and check size: 180 days
- Portfolio: 365 days
- Funding, hiring, launch, role-change, or explicit intent signal: 30 days
- Social post used as timing signal: 21 days
- Verified contact route: 30 days

Source trust tiers:

- Tier 1: connected provider record, verified platform ID, organization-owned registrable domain, regulated filing, or user-supplied authoritative source.
- Tier 2: reputable directory/provider with stable record ID and provenance.
- Tier 3: independently fetched public page or social post.
- Tier 4: search snippet or model extraction without a fetched source, never sufficient for identity or score eligibility.

A person identity needs one Tier 1 provider identity or two agreeing Tier 2/3 sources including a canonical profile. An organization identity needs an owned Tier 1 registrable domain or two agreeing Tier 2/3 identities. Exact-domain linking excludes shared hosts, marketplaces, profile hosts, accelerators, and portfolio directories; ownership must be observed, confidence remains reversible, and the registrable-domain normalization version is stored.

Show a human explanation, not four numbers, by default.

Editing a confirmed Mission creates a new Mission version. Existing matches retain their original reasoning and outreach history. Uncontacted entities may be asynchronously re-scored into a new match/version. Existing drafts are labeled with their source Mission version and invalidated only when recipient identity, objective, or a cited claim used in the draft changed. The match-inheritance rules in the entity model are normative for saved, dismissed, nurtured, drafted, sent, replied, converted, and stale rows.

## Security, Privacy, Permissions, and Compliance

### Context ingestion

- Accept HTTPS URLs only. Resolve DNS before and after redirects; block loopback, link-local, private, metadata-service, nonstandard-port, and credential-bearing URLs. Cap redirects at five.
- Accept PDF, DOCX, TXT, MD, and CSV in release one. Maximum 15 MB and 200 extracted pages. Reject encrypted archives and executable/container formats.
- Scan uploads before parsing. Parse in an isolated worker with CPU, memory, and wall-clock limits. Never execute macros, scripts, embedded objects, or remote document resources.
- Treat signed/private URLs as secrets. Redact query credentials from logs and UI. Fetch once, store a content hash and permitted extracted claims, and do not retain the signed URL.
- Raw pasted text and extracted file text are encrypted at rest, workspace-scoped, excluded from model training, and deleted after 30 days by default. Users can choose “retain source” for continuing refresh. Claims and citation hashes remain until the source or workspace is deleted.
- Evidence copied from a user source inherits that source's retention class. On expiry or deletion, excerpts are removed from both entity and match evidence, while permitted normalized claim summaries and keyed hashes remain for audit. Sensitive deck/file excerpts never become indefinite citations unless the user explicitly selects retain source. UI links stop resolving as soon as the source expires.
- Users can inspect, refresh, detach, and delete each context source. Deletion invalidates derived claims and enqueues affected Mission recompilation. Workspace deletion cascades to sources, claims, entities, matches, and provider identifiers.
- Every table uses workspace RLS. Service-role ingestion writes include explicit workspace ID and an audit event.

### Team workspaces

Release one inherits workspace visibility: owners and editors may create/edit Missions; viewers may view results and notes. Only owners and editors with connected sender identity may approve or send. Mission edits use optimistic version checks; conflicting edits create a new draft version for review rather than silently overwriting. Mission creator is recorded for audit, but Missions are shared by default.

### Personal data and outreach

- Store provenance and observed time for every personal identifier and contact point.
- Maintain a workspace and global `lead_suppressions` ledger keyed by provider ID, normalized email hash, LinkedIn ID, or other stable recipient identity.
- Honor do-not-contact, unsubscribe, deletion, and provider removal signals before drafting and again transactionally at send time.
- Contact routes include eligibility status and reason: verified, unverified, opted_out, deleted, channel_blocked, or policy_blocked.
- Retain the minimum public profile data required for the Mission. Deletion requests remove personal attributes and preserve only a suppression hash and compliance audit record.
- Hiring strategy does not ship in release one. Before it ships, prohibited/sensitive attribute handling and jurisdiction-specific employment review are separate launch gates.

Create `lead_recipient_identities` with `(workspace_id, entity_id, identity_type, normalized_hash, normalization_version, provider_id, status)`. Email and handle normalization is versioned; hashes use a server-held HMAC key, not an unkeyed digest.

Create `lead_suppressions` with recipient identity, scope (`workspace` or `product_enforcement`), reason, source, effective time, optional expiry, and audit metadata. Product-enforcement suppression prevents contact across workspaces without revealing that another workspace or user exists. Only the compliance service can read raw product-wide entries. Hash collisions are treated as blocked until manual compliance review; raw identifiers are not exposed cross-workspace.

Create `lead_send_ledger` with recipient identity, lead/match, Mission/version, channel, idempotency key, status, conversation ID, attempted/sent/replied/closed timestamps, and override audit ID. Unique `(workspace_id, channel, idempotency_key)` prevents duplicate attempts. `lead_send_overrides` records owner, conflict rule, reason, and time.

Approve performs a read-only eligibility preview. Send begins a database transaction that locks the recipient identity, checks suppressions and open conversations, applies the 30-day rule, inserts a pending ledger row, and commits before calling the provider. Provider completion updates that row idempotently. A provider timeout remains `unknown` and must be reconciled before retry; it is never blindly resent.

### Send conflict policy

A conflict exists when the same resolved recipient identity has an outbound message from the workspace within 30 days under another open Mission, or has any open conversation marked awaiting reply. At approve and send time, a transaction checks the recipient-level send ledger and suppression ledger. The UI warning alone is not enforcement. Owners may override the 30-day duplicate rule with a reason; suppression and open-conversation rules cannot be overridden.

## Preserving Existing Lifecycle Primitives

The following lifecycle primitives remain and should feel familiar:

- One unified feed
- Search, filters, save/follow, dismiss, import, and export
- Detail panel selection
- Draft, rewrite, polish, approve, and send
- LinkedIn verification and connection checks
- Notes and message thread
- Nurture playbook and next action
- Reply handling and conversion stages
- Signal-based resurfacing

Intentional behavior changes:

- Organization lead: resolve one or more relevant people before person-to-person outreach.
- Person lead: message the person directly; show their organization only as context.
- No contact path: recommend research, follow, content engagement, or save-for-later instead of presenting a dead send button.
- Multiple Missions: outreach and fit reasoning are scoped to the Mission match even when identity is shared.

### Consumer compatibility gate

Before production person writes, contract tests must pass for both entity types across every consumer:

| Consumer | Organization expectation | Person expectation |
|---|---|---|
| Feed/detail | Organization primary, person secondary | Person primary, organization secondary |
| Draft/polish | Resolve a recipient before drafting | Draft directly to target person |
| Verification | Verify selected contact | Verify target identity/contact route |
| Approve/send | Require eligible recipient | Require eligible target contact point |
| Import/export | Preserve company columns | Emit target type/name plus optional organization |
| Nurture | Organization match with person steps | Person match with direct steps |
| Reply matching | Contact/provider identifiers | Target/provider identifiers |
| Notes/messages | Match scoped | Match scoped |
| Conversion | Mission match conversion | Mission match conversion |
| Digest/analytics | Entity-aware label and counts | Entity-aware label and counts |

## States and Edge Cases

### No context

Allow the Mission. Ask for a one-sentence description only if the objective cannot be interpreted without it. Never block the lead surface behind global onboarding completion.

### Ambiguous Mission

Ask one discriminating follow-up. Example: “Do you want venture funds, individual angels, or both?” Do not open a general settings form.

### Zero results

Explain which constraints eliminated candidates. Offer one-click relaxations, such as adjacent geography or broader stage, with the exact change shown before rerunning.

### Many weak results

Do not fill the feed to appear productive. Show fewer targets and state that evidence was too weak for the rest.

### Duplicate target across Missions

Keep one canonical entity but separate Mission match, reasoning, status, and outreach context. Warn before sending a second conflicting message to the same person.

### Person changes companies

Preserve the person identity and relationship history. Re-score every active Mission affected by the move. Do not rename the person lead into the new company.

### Organization changes name or domain

Reuse the existing rename history and domain reconciliation. Canonical entity identity remains stable.

### Unsupported channel

Show the reachable channels and recommended non-send action. Never draft an email when no email exists or imply a LinkedIn profile was verified when it was not.

### Partial enrichment failure

Stream grounded targets as they become workable. Mark missing sections and allow retry without discarding reviewed results.

### Existing workspaces

Migrate their active ICP into a Mission named from its current profile. Existing leads remain visible under “Imported mission” or the migrated profile. No empty-feed reset.

Existing lead rows receive `trust_level = legacy_reviewed` when they have prior sent/replied/conversion activity and remain actionable for history and replies, but new outbound drafts require the new workable contract. Unsourced legacy rows become candidates. New CSV/paste imports create `user_asserted` candidates: user-provided identity fields count as provenance but not independent verification. They enter grounding/enrichment, become workable only after the normative contract passes, and preserve the original import row and mapping errors for audit. A workspace owner may manually confirm identity, but cannot bypass suppression or channel eligibility.

### Re-score fan-out

Claims record which Mission criteria depend on them. Context or affiliation changes enqueue only affected Mission/entity pairs. Jobs debounce for five minutes, coalesce duplicate dependencies, prioritize open conversations and selected Missions, cap at 1,000 pairs per workspace per hour, and mark affected matches stale until recomputed. Larger fan-out continues from a resumable cursor without blocking feed reads.

## Migration Plan

### Release-one boundary

Release one is the Mission composer, Leads-local context capture, customer-acquisition and fundraising strategies, people/organization results, claim-level citations, and the existing manual approval workflow. It does not ship global onboarding promotion, hiring/partnership/networking strategies, organization-to-organization graphs, automatic Mission mutation, new scheduling controls, or autonomous sending.

Release one launches through independent vertical gates:

1. Internal customer-acquisition organization flow, including context, Mission, grounded organization, resolved buyer, evidence, and existing approval workflow.
2. Customer-acquisition person compatibility after the full consumer matrix passes.

4. Feedback proposals after enough structured dismiss data exists. Recurring runs stay on the legacy scheduler until the new run model is proven manually.

Each slice can remain flagged off without blocking the prior slice. Public copy says “find customers and investors,” never “leads for anything,” until more strategy modules ship.

- Every flag combination used in rollback has an automated fixture: old organization, migrated organization, new organization, person candidate, and person workable lead.
- Contract cleanup occurs only after one stable release, zero reconciliation errors, and a signed rollback snapshot.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Minimal typed columns

Add `target_type` and person fields directly to `signal_leads`, keep discovery and identity otherwise company-centric.

Pros:

- Smallest diff
- Fastest initial person card
- Minimal schema work

Cons:

- Same person duplicated across sources and Missions
- Fit, identity, and workflow remain conflated
- Every new objective needs another company-centric exception
- High risk of person/company messaging mistakes

Completeness: 5/10.

### Approach B: Objective-driven engine with compatibility layer

Add durable context, Missions, canonical typed entities, entity relationships, and mission-scoped matches while retaining `signal_leads` as the existing workflow record.

Pros:

- Supports people, organizations, and multiple objectives correctly
- Preserves current lead actions and production data
- Allows incremental rollout and rollback
- Separates identity from why the target matters now

Cons:

- Requires a disciplined compatibility layer
- Transitional field duplication must be removed later
- Discovery and enrichment contracts change

Completeness: 10/10.

### Approach C: Separate people and organization products

Keep company leads untouched and create a separate people pipeline and UI.

Pros:

- Each pipeline can optimize for its entity type
- Lower immediate migration risk

Cons:

- Duplicate feed, scoring, evidence, outreach, nurture, analytics, and imports
- Users must decide which tool to use before they know the right target
- Cross-entity relationships become awkward

Completeness: 7/10.

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach B.

The product should feel like one familiar lead workflow, while the data model becomes honest about what a lead is: a typed entity matched to a current Mission. The compatibility layer is the key to reducing both user friction and migration risk.

## Success Criteria

### Activation

- At least 70% of first-time Leads visitors start a Mission without opening advanced settings.
- Median time from opening Leads to confirmed Mission is under 45 seconds.
- Global onboarding context step has a visible skip and causes no measurable increase in onboarding abandonment.

### Workability

- First grounded candidate appears within 30 seconds; five workable targets within 90 seconds at p50 only after the provider spike validates the latency budget.
- 100% of results in the actionable feed meet the normative workable-lead contract. Lower-quality results are labeled candidates and separated.
- Zero ungrounded target identities in automated evaluation and sampled production review.
- Fewer than 10% of top-five targets are dismissed as wrong person or wrong organization after the first tuning cycle.

### Continuity

- Existing lead rows, drafts, notes, messages, nurture stages, and conversion states remain accessible after migration.
- Existing organization lead draft/send tests remain green without source-specific UI forks.
- Person and organization targets use the same approve, save, dismiss, and nurture interaction model.

### Learning

- Mission-level dismiss reasons and positive actions are recorded.
- Users can see and undo every accepted feedback-derived Mission change.
- The system measures performance separately by objective, entity type, and source.

## Rollout and Safeguards

- Feature flag the Mission composer, generalized discovery, and typed feed separately.
- Dual-read existing leads through compatibility helpers before enabling person discovery.
- Shadow-run generalized ranking against current company results and compare ordering.
- Enable internal/demo workspaces first, then new workspaces, then migrated workspaces.

### Provider and latency gate

Before selecting a people-search provider, run a fixed benchmark of at least 100 queries, stratified equally across customer-acquisition decision makers, independent buyers, VC partners, and angels. Build the labeled set before provider runs from manually verified entities, with at least 25 expected entities per stratum. Measure top-10 recall, grounded identity precision, Mission-fit precision, duplicate rate, and usable citation coverage separately. A query batch is one Mission query plan capped at ten provider queries and 50 raw candidates. Launch requires:

- At least 70% top-10 recall against the locked hand-labeled target set in every stratum
- At least 90% grounded identity precision
- At least 80% Mission-fit precision and under 5% duplicate entities after conservative linkage
- Claim provenance that permits display and citation
- p95 provider latency under 12 seconds per query batch
- Cost below the approved cost-per-workable-lead ceiling
- Terms permitting the intended storage, enrichment, and outreach use

Target critical path is 75 seconds p50, leaving 15 seconds of operational headroom before the 90-second product target. Search and context lookup run in parallel; qualification fetches run concurrently with a maximum of five requests per provider and respect provider rate limits:

- Mission compile: 3 seconds
- Parallel context lookup plus candidate search: 15 seconds critical path
- Identity grounding: 10 seconds
- Parallel qualification fetch: 22 seconds
- Scoring/action selection: 5 seconds
- Top-result contact verification: 15 seconds
- Database/UI orchestration: 5 seconds

Cache source documents by URL and freshness policy. Stop provider work when the run budget is exhausted. If five workable targets cannot finish in 90 seconds, stream grounded candidates with honest state and continue the resumable run in the background. Never lower the workable contract to hit latency.

## Glossary

- **Current signal:** A strategy-recognized event observed within its configured freshness window and backed by a citation.
- **Valid next action:** A channel-eligible send action, or a concrete non-send action on a cited public surface, that passes suppression and workspace policy.
- **Warm path:** A verified mutual connection, prior conversation, follow relationship, or recorded engagement owned by the workspace.
- **Verified link:** A URL or provider identifier confirmed by the connected provider or two agreeing public sources.
- **Reachable:** At least one contact route is eligible at the current moment; merely possessing an email string is not enough.
- **Fresh:** The claim age is within the objective strategy's versioned freshness policy.
- **Candidate:** A grounded target missing one or more workable-lead requirements.
- **Workable lead:** A Mission match that passes identity, fit, timing, action, evidence, and safety gates.
