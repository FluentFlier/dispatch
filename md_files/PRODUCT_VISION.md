# Content OS -- Full Product Vision

> This is the full-picture reference document. Implementation phasing is tracked separately.
> When any agent or engineer loses context, start here.

---

## 0. One-Sentence Concept

A **calendar-aware, persona-driven Content OS** that turns your real-world events into platform-native content (LinkedIn and X first, Instagram and Threads next, Reddit last), orchestrates posting across channels, and helps you grow with analytics, suggestions, and safe automation -- so you can show up online without spending your life in social tools.

---

## 1. Who This Is For

**Primary -- Technical founders and builders**
People actively attending meetups, demos, talks, customer calls, and launches but too busy to craft thoughtful content for LinkedIn and X regularly.

**Secondary -- Solo creators and professionals**
Individuals who want a strong, consistent presence on LinkedIn and X but find planning, drafting, and scheduling across platforms mentally exhausting.

**Future -- Content teams around a founder**
Small teams where the founder's calendar, events, and voice live in one system and the team helps orchestrate content without bottlenecking on raw ideas.

Key mindset:
> "I live my life, go to events, talk to people. The system turns that into smart content and growth. I approve the important bits; everything else is handled."

---

## 2. Core Problems and Insight

### Problems We Solve

- People have high-signal experiences (events, calls, launches) but don't turn them into content before the memory fades.
- Existing tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout, Lately) assume you already have content or long-form assets. They help repurpose and schedule but don't capture life events as the starting point.
- Manual workflows are painful: export notes, dump into ChatGPT, rewrite to match your voice, manually paste into multiple schedulers, track analytics separately, guess what worked.
- Voice-matched tools (VoiceMoat) are single-platform. Multi-platform tools (Buffer, FeedHive) have no voice identity.

### Core Insight

The most defensible angle is **event to story to multi-platform content**, anchored in:

- Your **calendar** -- events as ground truth of what is happening in your life and work.
- Your **persona and voice** -- so content feels like you, not generic AI output.
- A **multi-platform brain** that knows how to tailor and schedule content natively across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, Threads, and Reddit.

Instead of "an AI caption generator," Content OS is:

> "The system that notices when something important happened in your life or work, asks you a few sharp questions, then turns that into an always-on presence across platforms and helps you grow it."

---

## 3. Product Pillars

**1. Context First, Not Prompt First**
Every generation is grounded in: calendar events, past high-performing posts, persona and pillars, and analytics. The AI acts like a teammate who knows your history, not a generic chatbot you have to re-brief every time.

**2. Persona Fidelity and Brand Safety**
Everything runs through a voice and persona model tuned per user, with strong guardrails and human approval. Automation augments; it does not replace human judgment. Every post has a voice match score before it is shown to the user.

**3. Multi-Platform Native Content**
Every output is tailored to the platform's format and norms -- threads vs carousels vs captions. Never blind copy-paste across platforms.

**4. Growth Brain, Not Just Scheduler**
The system does not just push content. It learns which event-derived posts, formats, and topics drive growth and translates that into simple, actionable suggestions. Hook intelligence feeds every generation.

**5. Human-in-the-Loop Automation**
Drafts and replies are suggested and queued, never silently deployed. No auto-posting without explicit approval.

## 4. Core Systems (Technical Differentiators)

These are the systems that make Content OS meaningfully different from every scheduling tool on the market. Any engineer building here must understand all five.

### 4.1 Voice Pipeline

Every piece of content generated by Content OS passes through a multi-step voice pipeline:

1. **Draft** -- Claude generates content from the user's prompt, event context, and persona.
2. **Evaluate** -- A second pass scores the draft on five voice metrics: authenticity, directness, specificity, pacing, and absence of AI tells. Returns a `voice_match_score` (0-100) and an `ai_score`.
3. **Revise** -- If the score is below threshold, the pipeline revises the draft with specific notes on what to fix.
4. **Humanize** -- Post-processing strips common AI tells (em dashes, filler transitions, corporate phrasing).

Every generation returns: final text, voice match score, AI score, whether a revision occurred, and the full evaluation matrix. This is the core quality guarantee.

### 4.2 Persona and Memory Architecture

The persona system is inspired by Ada (our AI secretary product) and builds a layered identity for each creator:

**Declared layer** (set by the user):
- Bio facts, content pillars, voice description, voice rules, vocabulary fingerprint (words they always or never use), structural patterns (sentence length, hook style, closing pattern).

**Inferred layer** (extracted by AI from the user's past posts):
- Writing tone, posting cadence per platform, average post length, platform-style notes, content interests.
- Extracted via a persona-extract function that imports from LinkedIn, X, or pasted writing samples.

**Long-term memory layer** (Supermemory):
- Every published post, event log, and performance signal gets stored as a memory entry.
- Semantic search retrieves relevant context at generation time: "user wrote about NVIDIA before, here is what they said."
- Memory improves every generation without the user doing any extra work.

**Story Bank** (manual memory):
- A scratchpad where the user drops raw memories, experiences, or ideas at any time.
- AI mines each entry into an angle, hook, script, and caption line.
- Acts as a permanent creative backlog that feeds the generation system.

### 4.3 Hook Intelligence System

The most technically sophisticated system in the codebase. Runs silently in the background on every generation.

- **Dataset:** 1000+ high-converting viral hooks mined from the top creators on LinkedIn and X via GStack (dev) and Apify (production). Living dataset that grows continuously.
- **Scorer:** Every hook is scored on conversion potential. The RL (reinforcement learning) trainer updates scores based on real engagement data from published posts.
- **Injection:** The top 6 hooks for the current context are injected into every generation as examples. The AI adapts the structure and energy of these hooks to the user's voice.
- **Agent tools:** Hook intelligence is exposed as callable tools so future agentic flows can query it directly.
- **Research Lab:** Visible in Analytics. Users can browse top-performing hooks, see what is trending by format and topic, and understand what patterns are working in their niche.

### 4.4 Event Capture System

The primary input mechanism for content creation:

1. **Calendar awareness:** Content OS reads Google Calendar and Notion Calendar. After a high-signal event ends, it triggers the capture flow.
2. **Event research:** The system reads the calendar event description, then runs a web search to gather additional context (speakers, announcements, agenda) before generating questions.
3. **Targeted Q&A:** 5 questions generated specifically for that event using the calendar context and web search findings. Not generic questions -- event-specific ones.
4. **Answer input:** User answers via text or voice (Whisper transcription runs in the background). Both inputs are converted to structured story data.
5. **Multi-platform drafts:** From one event + answers, the voice pipeline generates a LinkedIn post, X thread, and Threads post. Each is platform-native, not copy-pasted.

The event capture system replaces the "open a blank doc and try to remember what happened" workflow.

### 4.5 Voice Input and Audio Layer

**Input:** Whisper API transcribes spoken answers into text. The mic button sits alongside every text input -- optional, not a replacement. Particularly useful during or immediately after an event when typing is inconvenient.

**Output:** ElevenLabs Professional Voice Clone trained on the user's voice samples. Generates audio versions of posts for platforms that support audio or video content. Also used internally to verify generated drafts sound like the user before presenting them.

## 5. Feature Sets by Horizon

### Horizon 1 -- Event-to-Post OS (LinkedIn + X + Threads)

Goal: A technical founder can connect calendar and accounts and consistently publish strong LinkedIn and X posts driven by real events, with minimal friction.

- Account and calendar connection (LinkedIn, X, Google Calendar, Notion Calendar)
- Persona and voice setup (onboarding + import from past posts)
- Event capture and Q&A (calendar-triggered, 5 targeted questions, text + voice input)
- Draft generation with voice pipeline (LinkedIn, X thread, Threads)
- Review and approve flow (no auto-posting, ever)
- Story Bank (manual experience capture, always-on creative backlog)
- Hook Intelligence running silently on every generation
- Scheduling with optimal time suggestions
- Basic analytics per post and per event type
- Engagement inbox (AI-drafted replies to comments, human approval before send)
- ElevenLabs voice clone setup in Voice Lab

### Horizon 2 -- Cross-Platform Story Hub (+ Instagram)

Goal: Extend the same event and pillar logic to visual platforms.

- Instagram integration (event Q&A + uploaded photos turn into caption and carousel concepts)
- Media ingestion: attach raw photos or video to an event, generate Instagram-ready content
- Cross-platform repurposing: one event story generates LinkedIn post, X thread, Instagram carousel, Threads post
- Enhanced analytics: which platforms and formats drive follows, replies, and saves
- Content recycling: mark posts as high-performing, system proposes updated variants with new hooks

### Horizon 3 -- Full Content OS (+ Reddit, Playbooks, Automation)

Goal: Become the single content brain and operations layer for the founder.

- Reddit integration (discussion posts and comment replies tuned per subreddit)
- Playbooks and campaign templates (launch week, conference week, pre-built multi-post sequences around calendar events)
- Safe auto-reply for simple interactions (praise, basic questions) under user-defined rules
- A/B testing (test two hooks for similar event recaps, tie results back to events and pillars)
- Team collaboration (multiple users on one founder's persona, roles for founder vs team)
- Growth experiments (dial up or down automation, format experiments per platform)
- Deeper media workflow integration (auto-captioning, video cutdowns from event recordings)

## 6. Platform Rollout Order

1. LinkedIn (highest value per post for the professional and founder persona)
2. X / Twitter (highest velocity, best for threads and real-time takes)
3. Threads (easiest API, growing fast)
4. Instagram (requires image, strictest API -- supported but recommended last for new users)
5. Reddit (last -- requires subreddit awareness, different content norms entirely)

## 7. User Experience Narrative

**Step 1 -- Connect and define**
Connect LinkedIn, X, and Google Calendar. Define persona, pillars, and initial voice through a guided import of past posts. AI extracts inferred voice traits; user applies or dismisses suggestions.

**Step 2 -- Live life, attend events**
Go to the NVIDIA meetup, the customer call, the startup demo day. Content OS quietly ingests events from calendar and flags the high-signal ones.

**Step 3 -- Post-event capture**
After each event, the OS asks 5 targeted questions based on what it knows about that event. Answer by typing or speaking. Done in under 3 minutes while still fresh.

**Step 4 -- Multi-platform drafts**
OS generates LinkedIn post, X thread, and Threads post, all aligned with the user's voice and event context. Hook Intelligence picked the best opening structure. Voice pipeline scored and revised the drafts.

**Step 5 -- Review and approve**
The founder reviews drafts in one place, tweaks a line, approves. No silent posting.

**Step 6 -- Smart scheduling and publishing**
OS schedules posts at optimal times. The founder sees a simple calendar of upcoming content per platform.

**Step 7 -- Analytics and growth suggestions**
OS tracks how each event-generated post performs. Surfaces plain-language insights: "Event recap plus personal insight posts drive 2x more saves. Announcement-only posts underperform -- try adding a story hook."

**Step 8 -- Engagement and automation**
Safe replies are drafted and queued for approval. Complex comments come with suggested replies. The founder dials automation up or down based on comfort.

**Step 9 -- Expansion to media and playbooks**
When they start recording talks or taking event photos, OS helps turn those into cross-platform series. For big periods like a launch or conference, they pick a playbook and let OS orchestrate content sequences around their calendar.

At full maturity, Content OS is the founder's content brain and operations skeleton where events, persona, and growth all converge into one coherent system.

## 8. Architectural Patterns

**Context-first AI**
Always ground generation in calendar events, past high-performing posts, persona, pillars, and analytics. AI becomes "another teammate who knows your history," not a generic chatbot.

**Content multiplication**
From one core experience, generate many platform-specific artifacts. Track which story units were repurposed where to avoid duplicate posting.

**AI scheduling and operational health**
Use historical engagement data to pick optimal posting times per user and platform. Monitor queue health: are posts going out consistently, are replies getting handled?

**Intelligent engagement**
Sentiment and intent detection before any reply action. Auto-reply only for safe, simple interactions. Bubble up risky cases for human review.

**Cross-platform tailoring**
Treat each platform as distinct with different formats, lengths, and audience norms. Always adjust structure per channel.

---

## 9. Guiding Principles

- **Always respect human time and reputation.** Automation exists to reduce drudgery; approvals and guardrails protect the user's name.
- **Events and experiences drive the roadmap.** Calendar and what actually happened are core inputs; generic keyword prompts are secondary.
- **Voice and pillars are the source of truth.** Everything -- from captions to replies -- maps back to the user's defined persona and pillars.
- **Cross-platform, not platform-chaos.** The OS brings platforms into one coherent story, tailoring appropriately but keeping the message consistent.
- **Data-backed growth, not vibes.** Suggestions are grounded in observed performance and hook intelligence patterns.
- **Hook intelligence is always on.** Every generation benefits from what the system has learned about what converts, regardless of whether the user thinks about it.
